CopydoWell vs. Mailmeteor: which Google Sheets mail merge fits you?

By Metalix · July 18, 2026 · 6 min read

Both send personalized email from a Google Sheet, and both are good at it. But they're built around different priorities. Here's a fair look at where each one wins, so you can pick the right tool rather than the loudest one.

We make CopydoWell, so treat this as our perspective — but we've kept the facts about Mailmeteor accurate and pointed you to their site for anything that changes. If tracking is what you need, we say so plainly below.

Where Mailmeteor is strong

Mailmeteor is a mature, widely used mail merge tool with a genuinely good reputation. Credit where it's due:

  • Email tracking — opens and clicks, with campaign analytics. This is a core reason people choose it, and CopydoWell doesn't do it.
  • Deliverability focus — a lot of their product is built around getting mail to the inbox.
  • Maturity and scale — years of development and a very large user base.

On its free plan, Mailmeteor lets you send up to 50 emails per day, with attachments, scheduling, and tracking available on paid plans. Plans and prices change, so check Mailmeteor's pricing page for current details.

Where CopydoWell is different

CopydoWell optimizes for being free, private, and sitting right beside your sheet:

  • Free attachments and rich text. Attachments — including a different file per row from a column of Drive links — and bold/italic/color formatting are included, not paywalled.
  • Sends from your own Gmail quota. Rather than a fixed free-tier cap, your limit is Google's own daily sending quota (commonly ~500/day on Gmail, up to ~2,000/day on Workspace).
  • No server, no tracking. It runs on your Google sign-in via a send-only Gmail scope and sends nothing to any outside server — no tracking pixels.
  • It also copies data between sheets. This is the big one: CopydoWell isn't only mail merge. Its Copy Tool pulls, sorts, filters, and schedules syncs between spreadsheets — something a dedicated mail merge tool simply doesn't offer.

So which should you pick?

Choose Mailmeteor if tracked campaigns matter to you — you want to know who opened and clicked, and you're running enough volume that analytics and deliverability tooling earn their keep.

Choose CopydoWell if you want personalized email with attachments and formatting for free, prefer sending from your own Gmail with no third-party server in the loop, and you'd also benefit from a tool that moves and syncs data between spreadsheets. For a lot of recruiters, teachers, small businesses, and outreach teams, that combination covers the whole job in one place.

They're not mutually exclusive, either — some people use Mailmeteor when they need tracking and keep CopydoWell for everyday sends and all their sheet-to-sheet copying.

Frequently asked questions

Is CopydoWell or Mailmeteor better for mail merge?

It depends on what you need. Mailmeteor is a mature, tracking-focused tool with strong deliverability and a large user base — choose it if you need open/click tracking and campaign analytics. CopydoWell is a good fit if you want free attachments and rich text, want to send from your own Gmail quota rather than a capped free tier, and also need a tool that copies, sorts, and filters data between sheets.

What is Mailmeteor's free plan limit?

Mailmeteor's free plan lets you send up to 50 emails per day, with features like attachments, scheduling, and tracking reserved for its paid plans. Check Mailmeteor's pricing page for current details, as plans can change.

Does CopydoWell offer email tracking?

No. CopydoWell does not track opens or clicks — it's built around privacy and sending from your own Google account, with no tracking pixels or outside servers. If open/click tracking is essential to you, a tool like Mailmeteor is a better fit for that specific need.
